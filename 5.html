The Wit That Won Hearts – Answers

1. What lesson did you learn from the story?

The story teaches that calm thinking and cleverness can solve emotional problems. Tenali Rama’s wit resolved a misunderstanding without blame. We learn that assumptions harm relationships, while patience and empathy can heal them. Being kind and wise, even in difficult moments, can bring peace, happiness, and stronger emotional bonds.

2. What was the problem between the king and the queen in the story?

The king felt insulted when the queen yawned during his poem recital. He believed she disrespected his poetry and stopped speaking to her. In truth, the queen was simply tired. This small misunderstanding hurt their relationship deeply until Tenali Rama wisely intervened and helped the king understand her innocence.

3. How was the problem solved and by whom?

Tenali Rama solved the problem with humour and wisdom. He acted out a farming scene, connecting yawning to daily life. The king understood his mistake and recognised that yawning isn’t offensive. This made him apologise to the queen. Rama’s cleverness helped end the conflict and restore royal happiness.

4. What were the characteristics of Vijayanagara Empire while were popular?

The Vijayanagara Empire was known for its rich culture, art, literature, and architecture. During Krishnadeva Raya’s rule, it reached a golden age. Poets like Tenali Rama were celebrated, and wisdom was valued in court. Learning and creativity flourished, making the empire one of India’s greatest cultural and intellectual centers.
